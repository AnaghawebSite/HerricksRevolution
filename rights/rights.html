Right #1

Protesting Rights

One of the rights that you have are your PROTESTING RIGHTS. You have the right to protest against the British. The British have put many taxes against you, inlcuding the Sugar Act of 1764, the Stamp Act of 1765, the Townshend Acts of 1767, and many more. However, don't protest unless it's absolutely necessary. You don't want to bring up unnecessary conflicts with Britain!

Right #2

Government Rights

Another right that you have is to SELF GOVERN yourselves. Even though Britain is your mother country, you do not need to ask permission from Britain every time you create a law between yourselves. You can create your own laws and govern yourselves, independent of British control. You may also conduct meetings within yourselves, to talk about any issues within the colonies.

Right #3

Property Rights

Additionally, you have the right to own your property without interferance. For instance, you can carry weapons to defend yourself against sudden threats or dangers. You may also possess any other types of property, provided they don't pose as a threat to other nations.

Right #4

Freedom of Religion

Moreover, a right that you have is FREEDOM of RELIGION. Meaning, you have the right to practice your own religion without any interferences. This allows you to express your beliefs freely. This right also plays a big role in forming and shaping a sense of community between the 13 colonies.

Right #5

Freedom of Speech

The most important right you have is FREEDOM OF SPEECH. This right helps you to express your opinions, and it helps you share your ideas without any fear. This includes not only speech, but also the right to publish letters, newspapers, and other types of written work.
